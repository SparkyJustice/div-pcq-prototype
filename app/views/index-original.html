Petition stage

Applicant - Apply for a divorce

The 'petition' (D8)

  • Start page: Apply for a divorce
  • Resume a saved divorce application
  • Email: Application submitted
  • Landing page: Waiting for court to issue application
  • Email: Application issued
  • Landing page: Application issued

Acknowledgement of Service stage

Respondent - Respond to the divorce application

Acknowledgement of Service (AOS)

  • Letter: "Your wife has applied for a divorce"
  • Start page: Respond to a divorce
  • Landing page: Respond to a divorce
  • Paper journey: New forms for responding offline
  • Letter: "Your wife has applied for a divorce" (paper journey)
  • Letter: If a solicitor asks to submit the response (paper journey)
  • Landing page: "Your answer has been submitted" (if they chose to defend)

Co-respondent - Respond to the divorce

Applies to adultery cases where a co-respondent has been named

  • Letter: "You have been named in the divorce application..."
  • Letter (paper journey): "You have been named in the divorce application..."
  • Paper journey: New forms for responding offline
  • Start page: Respond to a divorce
  • Landing page: Respond to a divorce
  • Email: DN approved - date of hearing
  • Landing page: DN approved - date of hearing

Applicant - Husband/wife didn't respond to the application

The applicant must complete paper forms for these journeys

  • Email: "Your husband didn't respond"
  • Email: "Your application for deemed service has been accepted"
  • Email: "Your application for dispensed with service accepted"
  • Email: "The bailiff has successfully served your application"
  • Email: "The bailiff successfully served your application, but your husband never responded

Undefended route

Applicant - Apply for decree nisi

The application for 'DN' can take place if the respondent doesn't defend

  • Email: "Your husband has responded and will not defend"
  • Email: "Your husband has responded and consents to the divorce"
  • Landing page: "Apply for decree nisi"

Applicant - DN application accepted

The court accepts the DN application

  • Email: "Your application for decree nisi has been accepted" (Certificate of Entitlement)
  • Landing page: "Your application for decree nisi has been accepted"

Applicant - DN application refused

The court needs more information or has made a general order

  • Email: "Court asks for an amended application"
  • Email: "Court asks for more information"
  • Landing page: Application rejected

Defended route

Applicant - Husband/wife did not consent to divorce or admit adultery

The applicant must choose another reason for divorce (or in the case of adultery can continue at their own risk)

  • Email: "Your husband didn't admit the adultery"
  • Email: "Your husband didn't consent to the divorce"

Applicant - Husband/wife chose to defend the divorce

The respondent must complete paper forms for these journeys, but the applicant will still receive updates online

  • Email: "Your husband is defending, and the court is waiting for the answer"
  • Email: "Your husband's answer has been submitted"
  • Landing page: "Your husband's answer has been submitted"
  • Email: "Your husband's answer was not received in time by the court"

Decree nisi granted

Applicant - DN granted

Usually the applicant will be granted a decree nisi by the court - in rare situations they may need to amend their application

  • Email: "The decree nisi (court order) for a divorce was granted"

Respondent - Outcome of DN application

The court considers the case and makes a decision

  • Email: "Your wife has applied for a decree nisi"
  • Email: "Your wife's application for decree nisi has been accepted" (Certificate of Entitlement)
  • Landing page: "Your husband/wife's decree nisi application has been accepted"
  • Email: "The decree nisi (court order) for a divorce was granted"
  • Letter: "Your wife has had to amend their application"

Decree absolute

Applicant - Completing the divorce

After a wait of 6 weeks and a day they can apply for the decree absolute

  • Email: "You may now apply for your decree absolute"
  • Email: "You cannot apply for your decree absolute until your financial order has been finalised"
  • Email: "Your decree absolute has been granted, your divorce is now complete"

Respondent - Completing the divorce

If the applicant doesn't apply for a decree absolute after the 6 week wait, the respondent can do it themselves 3 months later

  • Email: "You may now apply for your decree absolute"
  • Email: "You cannot apply for your decree absolute until your financial order has been finalised"
  • Email: "Your decree absolute has been granted, your divorce is now complete"
