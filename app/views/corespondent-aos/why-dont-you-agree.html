Preventing the divorce because it will cause you hardship

As your {{ respsHusbandOrWife() }} used separation for 5 years as the reason for divorce, you can try to prevent it on the basis that it will cause you severe hardship.

The hardship could be financial (for example, you'll lose your rights to a widow's pension) or another type of hardship (for example, you'll be stigmatized for cultural or religious reasons).

You'll need to convince the court that the hardship will be severe.

The court will take your and your husband's individual circumstances and interests into acccount. It will also take into account the needs of any children if you have any, and anyone else who would be affected by a divorce.

Severe hardship is rarely used as a defence against divorce, and it's even rarer for the court to reject a divorce application because of it.

You'll need to make your case for hardship in another form (called the 'answer') within 21 days. You'll also have to pay another £245 fee.
