Getting an order to decide how your assets will be divided

Getting an order to decide how your assets will be divided

If you can't agree you can ask the court to make a financial order. This is a legally binding decision on how your assets will be shared, and whether either of you pays maintenance to the other person, or maintenance for any children you have. If the financial order ends all your financial ties to each other once you're divorced then it's known as a clean break order. You can apply for orders for yourself, and your children if you have any.

Making your agreement legally binding

When you agree how your assets will be shared, the only way to make your agreement legally binding is to get a financial order (also known as a consent order). If the consent order also ends all your financial ties to each other once you're divorced then it's known as a clean break order.

Getting a clean break order

Getting a clean break order

Even if you don't want to make any financial claim now, you may want to ask the court to make a financial order which ends any financial ties between you after you are divorced. This is known as a clean break order and can be used to prevent any financial claims against each other which you may have now or in the future.
