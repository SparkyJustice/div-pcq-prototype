You can apply for the following court services to help you continue with your divorce.

Apply to deliver the application in another way

You can ask for the application to be sent (served) in another way or at a different place, e.g. sent to their work or served through Facebook or an email.

How to apply: Fill in form D11 and send it to the court with a fee of £50.

Apply for a search of other government departments to get the address

You can apply for a search of other government departments to get the address.

How to apply: Contact the court for the form and pay a fee of £50.

Pay for a court bailiff to ‘serve’ the application

You can pay for a court bailiff to deliver the application to your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}. You'll still need to provide the court with their address. This may be suitable for you if you think that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} has received the application, but can't or won't respond.

How to apply: Fill in form D89 and send it to the court with a fee of £110 and:

  • a photo or written description of your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}
  • a copy of your divorce application

Apply to the court for a ‘deemed service’

If you know your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} received the application, but can’t or won’t respond, you can apply to the court for a ‘deemed service’.

You need to be able to prove that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} received the application (eg, you have a text message from him saying they received it).

How to apply: Fill in form D11 and send it to the court with a fee of £50.

Apply to the court to 'dispense with service'

Under exceptional circumstances, you can ask the court to ‘dispense with service’ (decide your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} doesn’t have to be served the divorce application).

It’s up to the court to decide whether to grant this. Before it can be considered, you’ll need to show that you’ve done everything you can to find them.

You can’t dispense with service if you’re using the ‘Separated for 2 years with consent’ reason for divorce.

How to apply: Fill in form D11 and form D13b and send them to the court with a fee of £50.
