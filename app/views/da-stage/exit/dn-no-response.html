The court usually needs to hear from both parties in a marriage before it can grant a divorce.

Warning If it's safe to do so, usually the simplest way to proceed is to contact your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} directly to find out why they haven't responded. They can still send in their response after the date for them to do so has passed.

If you can't get in contact with your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}, you may want to consider asking their family, friends and workplace if they have a new address, and provide that to the court.

If there's still no response using the original address, you have the following options available to you, depending on your situation.

I need to search government records for my {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}’s address (£50)

You can apply for a search of government databases (eg the electoral role) for your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}’s address. Your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} must live in England or Wales.

How to apply: Contact the court and ask for the form to be sent to you.

How to pay: Call the court to make the £50 payment by card (between 8.30am and 5pm) or include a cheque with your form.

If you're eligible for help with fees you'll need to apply again and get a new reference number.

If you asked the court to order your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} to pay your costs, then these extra costs will also be taken into account.

I believe my {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} lives at that address, but chose not to respond (£110)
Using a court bailiff

If you think that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} is deliberately not responding to the application you can pay for a court bailiff to deliver it to them.

If the bailiff succeeds in delivering the application to them, you'll be given an ‘endorsement of service'. If they still don’t respond 14 days after delivery, you can use the endorsement to continue without your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}'s response.

The bailiff service can only be used at addresses in England and Wales where delivery by post has already failed. If you have a new address for your husband, you’ll have to get it delivered by post first before you can apply for the service.

How to apply: Fill in form D89.

You'll also need to send the following:

  • a photo or written description of your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}
  • a copy of your divorce application

Print and send the form and documents to the court's address at the top of this screen.

How to pay: Call the court to make the £110 payment by card (between 8.30am and 5pm) or include a cheque with your form.

If you're eligible for help with fees you'll need to apply again and get a new reference number.

If you asked the court to order your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} to pay your costs, then these extra costs will also be taken into account.

Using a process server

You can also hire a private 'process server' (someone who professionally delivers court orders and legal documents) to deliver the application. A solicitor should be able to give you the contact details for a local company.

I have evidence that my {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} received the application, but can't or won't respond (£50)

If you know your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} received the application, but can’t or won’t respond, you can apply to the court for a ‘deemed service’. This means that the court will decide that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} has received the application, and that you can continue without their response.

You need to be able to prove that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} received the application (eg, you have a text message from them saying they received it).

I’ve tried every possible way of delivering the application (£50)

Under exceptional circumstances, you can ask the court to ‘dispense with service’ (decide your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} doesn’t have to be served the divorce application).

It’s up to the court to decide whether to grant this. Before it can be considered, you’ll need to show that you’ve done everything you can to find them by contacting their:

  • relatives (including children)
  • friends
  • last known employer
  • trade union or professional organisation

You'll also need to have tried to find their address by searching for it on government databases.
