Read more about costs orders

How much you may have to pay

The applicant's costs usually include the £550 fee. They could also include other court fees and costs. The costs could be much higher if your husband has legal fees (eg a solicitor) and these are also included.

What happens now

Your husband will be asked to confirm that they want to claim their costs when they apply for a decree nisi.

The court will then make its decision on whether you should pay any costs. It will take all your circumstances into account, including your conduct, and your husband's conduct, before and during the divorce.

You'll be given the court's decision at the same time as you find out if the divorce will be granted, and the date of the hearing that the decree nisi will be made.

Objecting to the costs order

You can dispute the costs order if you don't think that you should pay it. You'll need to send a letter to the court stating why you don't think you should pay at least 14 days before the hearing.
