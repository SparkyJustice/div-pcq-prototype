Stages of the decree

The order (known as a decree) is made in 2 stages. You’re only divorced once both stages are complete.

  1. 1. The 'decree nisi', which shows that the court agrees that you’re entitled to a divorce.
  2. 2. The ‘decree absolute’, which legally ends the marriage. It cannot be made until at least 6 weeks after the decree nisi.

The court can cancel (known as rescind) the decree nisi at any time up until the decree absolute, if it's presented with new evidence showing why you shouldn’t be given a divorce.

The court will also order your husband to pay you all of the costs of the divorce.

The hearing

If you want to attend please email the court before the date of the hearing. If you, or your husband, want to dispute a costs order, the court must receive a letter stating the reasons for doing so at least 14 days before the hearing date.

You can find the court address and the time of the hearing on the ‘certificate of entitlement to a decree nisi’ which can be downloaded from your account: https://www.divorce.reform.hmcts.net/sign-in

After the hearing, you’ll both be sent an email telling you when you can apply for your decree absolute.
