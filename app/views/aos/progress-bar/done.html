What to do now

To defend the divorce you need to submit the 'answer to a divorce application' form. The deadline for this is 21 days from the date that you first signed in to respond to the application.

Download and fill in the paper form.

Post the form to:

  • East Midlands Regional Divorce Centre
  • PO Box 10447
  • Nottingham
  • NG2 9QN

You'll have to pay a £245 fee when you submit the form.

You'll probably have to go to a hearing with your {{ respsHusbandOrWife() }} to try to come to an agreement over the divorce.

If you don't submit your answer before the deadline, then your {{ respsHusbandOrWife() }} can continue the divorce as if you hadn't said that you intended to defence the divorce.

What happens next

{% if session.aos.consent == "No" %}

As you haven't given your consent, the divorce won't be able to continue based on the 2 years separation with consent 'fact' (reason for divorce).

The application will be returned to your {{ respsHusbandOrWife() }}. If they choose to continue, they will need to change the reason for divorce to one that doesn't need your consent.

Your {{ respsHusbandOrWife() }} will have to pay £95 to change the reason. You may have to pay some or all of this extra fee if they've applied to claim their divorce costs.

You'll be contacted by the court when your {{ respsHusbandOrWife() }} has made their decision.

If you decide that you want to consent to the divorce

You can change your mind and give your consent to the divorce at any time. You'll need to do this in writing. Contact the divorce centre if you want to do this.

  • change their reason for divorce (and pay a fee of £95)
  • provide evidence of the adultery

You'll be contacted by the court when your {{ respsHusbandOrWife() }} has made their decision.

{% else %}

Your response will be checked and then sent to your {{ respsHusbandOrWife() }}. They can then apply for the court order which will divorce you.

The order (known as a decree) is made in 2 stages. You’re only divorced once both stages are complete.

  1. The 'decree nisi', which shows that the court agrees that you’re entitled to a divorce.
  2. The ‘decree absolute’, which legally ends the marriage. It cannot be made until at least 6 weeks after the decree nisi.

You'll be sent an email after each stage.

The court can cancel (legally known as rescind) the decree nisi at any time up until the decree absolute, if it's presented with new evidence showing why you shouldn’t be given a divorce.

Contact the divorce centre if you don't hear anything back after 6 weeks.
