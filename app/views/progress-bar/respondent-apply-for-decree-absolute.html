Read more about the decree absolute

The court order for divorce (known as a decree) is made in 2 stages. You’re only divorced once both stages are complete.

  1. The 'decree nisi', which shows that the court agrees that you’re entitled to a divorce.
  2. The ‘decree absolute’, which legally ends the marriage. The applicant can apply for it 6 weeks after the decree nisi has been granted. The respondent can apply for it after a further 3 months have passed.

The court can cancel (legally known as rescind) the decree nisi at any time up until the decree absolute, if it's presented with new evidence showing why you shouldn’t be given a divorce.
