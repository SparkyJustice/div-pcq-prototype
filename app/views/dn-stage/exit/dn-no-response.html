The court usually needs to hear from both parties in a marriage before it can grant a divorce.

Warning If it's safe to do so, usually the simplest way to proceed is to contact your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} directly to find out why they haven't responded. They can still send in their response after the deadline has passed.

If you can't get in contact with your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}, you may want to consider asking their family, friends and workplace if they have a new address, and provide that to the court.

If there's still no response using the original address, you have the following options available to you, depending on your situation.

Which situation applies to you?

I have another residential address or a solicitor's address for my {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} (no fee)

Contact the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre to provide a different address for your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}. This can be a UK or international address.

You don't have to pay anything extra to have the application sent to a different address.

I need to send the application to a non-residential address, eg a work address (£50)

You can ask for the application to be sent to a non-residential address.

How to apply: Download and complete form D11. Send the form to the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre.

How to pay: Call the service centre to make the £50 payment by card (between 8.30am and 5pm) or include a cheque with your form.

If you're eligible for help with fees you'll need to apply again and get a new reference number.

If you asked the court to order your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} to pay your costs, then these extra costs will also be taken into account.

I need to search government records for my {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}’s address (£50)

You can apply for a search of government databases (eg the electoral role) for your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}’s address. Your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} must live in England or Wales.

How to apply: Contact the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre and ask for the form to be sent to you.

How to pay: Call the service centre to make the £50 payment by card (between 8.30am and 5pm) or include a cheque with your form.

If you're eligible for help with fees you'll need to apply again and get a new reference number.

If you asked the court to order your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} to pay your costs, then these extra costs will also be taken into account.

I need to send the application to my {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}'s email address (£50)

You can ask for the application to be delivered by email.

How to apply: Download and complete form D11. Send the form to the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre.

How to pay: Call the service centre to make the £50 payment by card (between 8.30am and 5pm) or include a cheque with your form.

If you're eligible for help with fees you'll need to apply again and get a new reference number.

If you asked the court to order your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} to pay your costs, then these extra costs will also be taken into account.

I believe my {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} lives at that address, but chose not to respond (£110)

If you think that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} is deliberately not responding you can pay a professional to deliver (serve) the divorce papers directly to them.

You can do this by using either a:

  • Process server (a privately hired professional)
  • Court bailiff (an employee of the courts)
Process servers

Contact a local solicitor who should be able to give you the contact details for a local company.

Court bailiffs

The bailiff service can only be used at addresses in England and Wales where delivery by post has already failed. If you have a new address for your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}, you’ll have to get it delivered by post before you can apply for the bailiff service.

If the bailiff succeeds in delivering the application, you'll be given a letter as evidence that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} has received the application. This is known as an ‘endorsement of service'. If your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} doesn’t respond within 14 days of getting the application, you can use this letter to continue your divorce without their response.

How to apply: Download and complete form D89.

You'll also need to send the following:

  • a photo of your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }}
  • a copy of your divorce application

Send the form to the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre.

How to pay: Call the service centre to make the £110 payment by card (between 8.30am and 5pm) or include a cheque with your form.

If you're eligible for help with fees you'll need to apply again and get a new reference number.

If you asked the court to order your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} to pay your costs, then these extra costs will also be taken into account.

I have evidence that my {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} received the application, but can't or won't respond (£50)

If you know your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} received the application, but can’t or won’t respond, you can apply to the court for a ‘deemed service’. This means that the court will decide that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} has received the application, and that you can continue without their response.

You need to be able to prove that your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} received the application (eg, you have a text message from {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} saying they received it).

I’ve tried every possible way of delivering the application (£50)

Under exceptional circumstances, you can ask the court to ‘dispense with service’ (decide your {{ petsHusbandOrWife() }} doesn’t have to be served the divorce application).

It’s up to the court to decide whether to grant this. Before it can be considered, you’ll need to show that you’ve done everything you can to find them by contacting their:

  • relatives (including children)
  • friends
  • last known employer
  • trade union or professional organisation

You'll also need to have tried to find their address by searching for it on government databases.

How to apply: Download and complete form D11 and form D13b. Send the forms to the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre.

How to pay: Call the service centre to make the £50 payment by card (between 8.30am and 5pm) or include a cheque with your form.

If you're eligible for help with fees you'll need to apply again and get a new reference number.
